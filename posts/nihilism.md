# think of it.
Why are you alive?

Just try answering that. What is the meaning or purpose of one's life?

To a person who is nihilistic, the answer would be: nothing.

Most philosophical thought is optimistic. It assumes there is hope in life, as if life has some inherent meaning. Nihilism, on the other hand, is a philosophical stance which holds that nothing in the world has any meaning, value, or purpose. It is like a void with no direction, no sign, and no purpose.

There are a few different types of nihilism. Existential nihilism is the belief that life has no inherent meaning or purpose. Moral nihilism rejects the idea of absolute moral or ethical values. Political nihilism is the rejection of existing political and social institutions, often believing they should be dismantled without necessarily proposing a replacement (I think I'm one too). Cosmic nihilism is the idea that the universe is completely indifferent, unintelligible, and blind to human existence, values, and concerns. and there a lot more too!

Knowingly or unknowingly, one might turn nihilistic in a lot of ways. But according to me, it's usually due to the loss of faith or belief in something. Say a person loses faith in God or religion and becomes an atheist. He no longer has a predefined framework or guide on how to lead his life. When people lose faith in the belief systems they grew up with, they often arrive at conclusions such as atheism, nihilism, or at least agnosticism.

But I don't think this is the way to live life.

Just think about it. What if everyone in the world were nihilists? They would work towards nothing, and eventually humanity itself would collapse. Though I am an atheist, I do believe religion has played a role in keeping people doing what they have to do, contributing to the development of the world instead of simply letting things die.

Friedrich Nietzsche, the famous philosopher and writer, criticized nihilism and warned that without traditional pillars, humanity would plunge into profound despair, apathy, and aimlessness.

In my point of view, nihilism is not a philosophy to live with. I see it more as a point in life where you truly become aware of yourself. It is the moment where you stop relying on borrowed meanings and start looking forward to living life in your own way.

I was a nihilist at one point. But instead of staying there, I tried to come out of it by building my own beliefs and my own way of living.

You build your own belief system. Your own sense of right and wrong.

Now, if someone asks me, "What are you living for?", I actually have an answer.
To do whatever the fuck I want to do.
To fulfill the things I've always wanted to do. It doesn't have to be anything big. Sometimes it's the smallest things that we ignore for no reason.

I've always wanted to climb onto a sunshade (or whatever the fuck it's called) and just sit there for a while. It serves no purpose. I just want to do it.

That's the point.

The life of a nihilist is basically an empty land. And once you're aware of that, you can build whatever the fuck you want on that land, without social pressure or any of that bullshit.
